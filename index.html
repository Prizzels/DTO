Introduction & Installation

Daelen's Testament of the Otherworldly recounts the incredible journey of a paladin to battle evil in a far off world called Golarion. It is composed of two volumes:

Volume 1, "Otherworldly Archetypes" - 12 new subclasses designed to blend into the original line up seamlessly.
Volume 2, "Codex Of Might and Magic" - A codex of new tactics, metamagic and reaction based teamwork to diversify your tactics and make combat more lethal.

These mods are an attempt to Pathfinderize the BG3 experience, bringing over some of my favorite mechanics in a 5e friendly way. Preview the new subclasses or Vol.2: Of Might and Magic by selecting any of the icons in the top bar.

Installation Notes
  • Volume 1 has minimal overrides and should be compatible with most mods but load it after any other class/skills mods just to be sure. It is recommended to use Mystra's or 5E Spells, especially for Dream Domain and Wretched Soul.
  • Volume 2 has considerable overrides and changes the game fundamentally, load it after anything that changes class/skills/combat.
  • It is intended that players use both mods to get the full experience but they are otherwise standalone.
  • It is safe to remove Volume 1 during a playthrough provided you respec characters to 1 and do not choose any of the added archetypes then save and unload the mod.
  • It is less safe to remove Volume 2 during a playthrough but following the above procedure is your best bet.

About This Mod

Codex of Might and Magic expands Baldur’s Gate 3 combat by introducing new reactions, teamwork mechanics, and spell-shaping options that make positioning and timing matter far more in every encounter. Martial characters gain more ways to punish mistakes and support allies outside their own turns, while casters gain broader access to metamagic-style spell customization. Healing, enemy behaviour, and several class features have also been reworked to make fights faster, deadlier, and more interactive. The goal is a battlefield where awareness, coordination, and build identity meaningfully change how combat unfolds.

General

Death's Door

Passive
When you drop to 0 hit points for the first time in combat and then regain hit points or become stable, you find yourself on Death's Door. The next time you drop to 0 hit points, you die instantly without making death saving throws. Removed by a Long Rest.

Ranged Reaction Attack|Action
You can ready a ranged attack against the next foe that moves into your line of sight within 18 m.

Opportunity Attacks

For both players and NPCs, Opportunity Attacks are now granted against spell casts and ranged weapon attacks.

As a result of this change, the Mageslayer feat now grants a 9 m teleport to the target before attacking.

Enemy Adjustments

Enemies can now use any special attack on their equipped weapon.
Some enemies are now Mageslayers — casters beware!
Intelligent foes now have access to feats.
Lethality
Passive — Enemies Only
  • Level 5+: Enemies roll an additional die on critical hit.
  • Level 11+: Reduces the number needed to roll a critical strike by 1.
  • Level 17+: Rolls two additional critical damage dice.

Healing Changes

Most healing spells have had their healing dice doubled. Lay on Hands gains 50% more dice.
  • Cure Wounds — Now has a base range of 9 m.
  • Prayer of Healing — Useable in combat, now heals all creatures in the radius, including enemies.
  • Revivify / Resurrection — Now brings targets back with 10% of their maximum hit points instead of 1.
